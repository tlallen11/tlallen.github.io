WHAT IS TERRAFORMING?

Terraforming is the Terraformation of turning a celestial body, such as a planet or our own moon into a ecosystem to sustain earth like lifeforms. This process can take as short as 50 years to as long as 100 million years.


THE THREE MAIN LEVELS OF TERRAFORMING

Para-terraforming

Humans can maintain a positive pressure chamber along with agricultural zones on the surface of a terrestrial body without atmospheric modifications.

Partial terraforming

Plants can grow on the terrestrial body's surface. Humans can walk on the surface with as little as simple air-breathing equipment. The terrestrial body has an ambient pressure similar to that of the earth

Full Terraforming

The terrestrial body is finally earth-like through vast transformations of the body's atmosphere and environment. Humans, plants, and animals can survive without any assistance.


WHY IS TERRAFORMING IMPORTANT?

As climate change is rapidly approaching, humans need to start searching for another home. Scientists predict that by 2030, we will no longer be able to stabilize the rate at which the global temperate rises. With that estimate, the earth will be inhabitable within 2-3 hundred years.

Even if climate change wasn't an issue, the earth's death is sadly inescapable. In about 7.56 billion years, the sun will exhaust the hydrogen in its core and start burning helium; likely engulfing the earth or making it completely uninhabitable.
